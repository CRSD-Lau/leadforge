Executive Summary
The opportunity, fully costed.

LeadForge AI is a one-person micro-agency powered by a Claude agent that executes a complete outbound sales cycle every weekday morning in under 15 minutes. The agent finds businesses without websites, sends personalised cold outreach, handles replies, and routes qualified leads to Neil Mitchell — who delivers a professional React / Next.js site in 3–5 days for a flat $650.

The model has real overhead: the Claude Max 20x plan at $200/month is the operational backbone, covering both the daily agent runtime and Claude Code for the site builds. At 3 sites/month, total monthly overhead runs approximately $212, leaving a net cash profit of ~$1,738 — an 89% margin. That margin improves with scale, since the $200 Claude plan is a fixed ceiling regardless of build volume.

The primary market is the Greater Saint John region in New Brunswick, where approximately 3,000 active businesses operate — many of them micro-enterprises with fewer than five employees and no meaningful web presence. With an estimated 35% of Canadian small businesses lacking an effective website, the local serviceable market exceeds 800 businesses within driving distance.

Market Opportunity
Saint John & New Brunswick: A massive, untapped local market.

Canada has over 1.2 million small businesses, with 98.1% classified as small. New Brunswick had approximately 17,300 active businesses as of late 2025, and the Saint John region accounts for just under 3,000 of those. The city's population of 130,000 supports a concentrated market of service-based micro-enterprises — restaurants, salons, trades, professional services — many of which operate without any web presence.

Nationally, an estimated 27–35% of small businesses still don't have a website. For micro-businesses with fewer than five employees, that figure rises to nearly 60%. In Atlantic Canada, digital adoption lags behind Ontario, BC, and Alberta, creating an even wider gap. The CFIB's September 2025 survey found that nine in ten businesses that do maintain a website use it primarily for information rather than sales — meaning even those with websites often have basic, outdated, or ineffective ones.

The competitive landscape for web design in New Brunswick is thin. Clutch lists only a handful of web design firms in the province, most charging $10,000+ for basic projects. Local freelancer supply is minimal. One regional competitor advertises sites starting at $380 with government ABF grant subsidies — confirming there are buyers at this price point, but supply is severely constrained.

Overhead & Rate Limits
The real cost of running on Claude.

This model depends on Anthropic's infrastructure at two points: the daily agent runtime and the site build via Claude Code. Both are covered by one plan — but that plan has hard limits that must be factored honestly.

Claude Max 20x ($200/month) provides an estimated 240–480 hours of Sonnet 4 and 24–40 hours of Opus 4 per week, governed by a 5-hour rolling window and a weekly ceiling (introduced August 2025). Claude Code sessions for full site builds are token-heavy and consume capacity faster than simple chat.

Six Mitigation Strategies

Time Separation

Run agent at 6:00 AM. Schedule Claude Code builds at 12:00 PM+ to avoid competing for the same 5-hour rolling window.

Model Selection

Use Sonnet 4 for agent tasks and most scaffolding. Reserve Opus 4 for complex decisions only.

Stagger Builds

Don't stack multiple builds on the same day. At 3 sites/month, space builds — one per week maximum.

Monitor Logs

Check ~/.claude/ session logs regularly. Third-party tools can parse token counts.

Overflow Budget

Maintain a $30–$50/month API overage buffer. At scale, evaluate migrating the agent to pure API calls.

5x as Fallback

If volume stays below 2 builds/month in the first 60 days, Max 5x at $100/mo may suffice — saving $100/mo.

Legal & Ownership Model
Clear terms. No grey areas.

1. Code Ownership Until Payment

LeadForge AI retains full ownership of the codebase until $650 is received. The client gets a Vercel preview during review but no code transfer until payment clears.

2. Domain Purchased by Client

The client purchases their own domain. Neil assists with DNS configuration at no extra charge as part of the launch process.

3. Site Transfer After Payment

On payment confirmation, Neil transfers the Vercel project, GitHub repo, and all assets. The client controls their deployment and hosting thereafter.

4. Hosting Handled by Neil or Client

Client may retain Neil's Vercel account or transfer to their own. Vercel's free tier covers all small business traffic needs.

5. CASL Compliance (Cold Email — Canada)

All cold outreach complies with Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation. Each email includes sender identification, a business address, and an opt-out mechanism. B2B prospecting to business email addresses falls under CASL's business card exemption when handled correctly.

Marketing & Lead Acquisition
The agent is the floor, not the ceiling.

Cold email is the automated foundation — but it's only one channel. A diversified acquisition strategy turns LeadForge AI from a cold outreach experiment into a discoverable, credible business. The agent's runtime should expand from 15 minutes to 60 minutes to accommodate these additional channels.

Expanded Agent Runtime — 60 Minutes

Increasing the daily agent window from 15 to 60 minutes unlocks four additional capabilities beyond cold email. GitHub Actions free tier still covers this comfortably. The agent runs sequentially: lead discovery and outreach first, then social engagement, then follow-ups.

1. Social Lead Mining (15 min/day)

The agent scans Facebook Groups, local community boards (Saint John Buy & Sell, NB Small Business Network), and Google Business listings for businesses posting without a website link. It flags these as warm leads — they're already active online but missing the one piece that would make them findable. The agent auto-drafts a personalised DM or comment for Neil to review and send.

2. Follow-Up Sequences (10 min/day)

Day 3 and Day 7 automated follow-ups for non-responders. The agent checks the lead database for stale "Sent" statuses, drafts a follow-up with a different angle (mockup offer, social proof, urgency), and sends. Industry benchmarks show follow-ups double response rates from 2–3% to 5–7%.

3. Portfolio Case Study Generation (10 min/day)

After each completed build, the agent auto-generates a short case study: business name, category, before/after, build timeline, and a testimonial request template. These feed directly into the LeadForge AI website and social content pipeline.

4. Google Business Profile Audit (10 min/day)

The agent identifies businesses with incomplete or low-rated Google Business Profiles and includes a GBP optimisation offer in the outreach — a natural upsell at $75/hr that demonstrates immediate value before the website conversation even starts.
